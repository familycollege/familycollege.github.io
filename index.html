Curriculum Design

We offer a tailored curriculum design that aligns with each student’s workload and interests, starting as early as 6th grade. This customized plan includes guidance on selecting the right classes, such as handpicked Algebra 1 class locally or online class that we believe give you the best results, to prepare for science competitions and specific problem-solving strategies. Science competitions can begin as early as 6th grade, giving students a head start on building a strong academic portfolio. By 7th grade, students will have the opportunity to participate in targeted extracurricular activities with our guidance. The experience will be a key element of their college applications. At the end of 7th grade, students can begin taking recommended community college courses to accelerate their academic progress. This early exposure will help boost their AP scores in high school and reduce the time needed to complete college. With careful planning and our support, students could earn an Associate’s Degree (AS) by the end of 9th grade, saving both time and money if they choose to attend top public universities like UC Berkeley or UCLA.

Latest Science Competition News

Intel International Science and Engineering Fair (ISEF) Winners Announced

  • Over 1,800 young scientists from more than 75 countries participated in this prestigious competition.
  • The top award went to Sarah Lee from California for her research on renewable energy storage systems.
  • John Patel (USA) was awarded for his groundbreaking research in microbiome studies, focusing on new treatments for antibiotic-resistant bacteria.
  • Alicia Gomez (Mexico) was recognized for her work in quantum computing, proposing a novel algorithm for data encryption.
  • The competition continues to inspire young minds to tackle global challenges through science and engineering.

European Union Contest for Young Scientists (EUCYS) 2024 Concludes in Brussels

  • The grand prize was awarded to a German team for developing a sustainable method to synthesize biodegradable plastics.
  • The event featured a diverse range of projects in physics, chemistry, biology, and engineering.
  • Participants demonstrated innovative approaches to solving environmental and scientific challenges.

Google Science Fair 2024 Winners Announced

  • The top prize went to Arjun Desai from India for his project on using machine learning models to predict and mitigate climate change effects.
  • Other standout projects included advancements in medical technology and sustainable agriculture.
